California Maki

Description:

This sushi roll is famous for its "inside-out" style (Uramaki), where the rice is on the outside and the seaweed (nori) is on the inside.
This technique hides the seaweed texture, making it perfect for sushi beginners.

Ingredients:

The Rice

  • 2 cups Sushi rice (short-grain Japanese rice), cooked
  • ¼ cup Rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
  • 1 tsp Salt (Note: You can also buy pre-made "Sushi Vinegar" to replace the vinegar/sugar/salt mix)

The Fillings

  • Imitation Crab (Surimi):8 sticks. Shred them by hand or fork and mix with 1 tbsp of Japanese mayonnaise (Kewpie) for creaminess.
  • Avocado: 1 ripe but firm avocado, sliced into thin strips.
  • Cucumber: ½ cucumber, seeded and julienned (cut into thin matchsticks).

The Essentials

  • Nori (Seaweed): 4 sheets, cut in half crosswise (you will use half-sheets).
  • Toppings: Toasted white sesame seeds or Tobiko (orange flying fish roe).
  • Condiments: Soy sauce, pickled ginger (gari), and wasabi.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Sushi Rice

    While the rice is still hot, gently fold in the mixture of rice vinegar, sugar, and
    salt using a wooden paddle. Fan the rice as you mix to cool it down to room temperature.
    The grains should look glossy and sticky, not mushy.

  2. Prepare the Work Station
    • Wrap your bamboo rolling mat (makisu) completely in plastic wrap. This is crucial
      because the sticky rice will be on the outside of the roll and will otherwise stick to the bamboo.
    • Prepare a small bowl of water with a splash of vinegar (called Tezu) to dip your fingers in; this prevents rice from sticking to your hands.
  3. Spread the Rice

    Place a half-sheet of nori on the mat, rough side facing up.

    • Dip your fingers in the Tezu water.
    • Grab a handful of rice (about the size of a lemon) and spread it gently over the entire surface of the nori. It should be a thin, even layer.
    • Sprinkle the rice generously with toasted sesame seeds or tobiko.
  4. The "Inside-Out" Flip

    Carefully flip the nori sheet over so that the rice is now facing down against the plastic-wrapped mat, and the bare nori is facing up.

  5. Add Fillings

    Arrange your ingredients in a horizontal line across the lower third of the nori sheet:

    • Lay down the cucumber strips first for a solid base.
    • Add the avocado slices next to them.
    • Top with the crab/mayo mixture.
    • Tip: Do not overfill, or the roll will burst.
  6. Roll
    • Lift the edge of the bamboo mat closest to you.
    • Roll it forward, using your fingers to tuck the fillings in tightly as you go.
    • Continue rolling until the rice meets the rice on the other side.
    • Give the mat a gentle squeeze to shape the roll into a cylinder (or squareish) shape.
  7. Cut And Serve
    • Transfer the roll to a cutting board.
    • Using a very sharp knife (wipe the blade with a wet cloth between cuts to prevent sticking),
      slice the roll in half.
    • Place the halves side-by-side and cut them into 3 or 4 even pieces (making 6 or 8 bites
      total).
    • Serve immediately with soy sauce and wasabi.
