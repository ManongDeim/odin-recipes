Yangyeom Chicken

Description:

This is the iconic sweet, spicy, and sticky fried chicken that put Korean street food on the global
map. Unlike American fried chicken, the batter relies heavily on potato or corn starch for a thin,
glass-like crust that stays crispy even when tossed in the thick sauce.

Ingredients:

The Chicken & Marinade

  • 2-3 lbs Chicken wings, drumettes, or boneless thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces).
  • 1 tsp Salt.
  • ½ tsp Black Pepper.
  • 1 tbsp Mirin or rice wine (to remove poultry odor).
  • 1 tsp Ginger, minced.

The Cripsy Coating

  • ½ cup Potato starch (preferred) or Cornstarch.
  • ¼ cup All-purpose flour.
  • ½ tsp Baking soda.
  • 1 Egg (optional, makes the batter slightly richer).
  • Oil: Neutral oil (canola, vegetable, or grapeseed) for deep frying.

The Yangyeom Sauce

  • 3-4 cloves Garlic, minced.
  • ¼ cup Gochujang (Korean red chili pepper paste).
  • 1/3 cup Ketchup (adds tanginess).
  • 1/3 cup Rice syrup or Corn syrup (essential for the glossy, sticky shine).
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce.
  • 1 tbsp Sugar.
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil.

The Garnish

  • Toppings: Toasted sesame seeds or crushed peanuts.
  1. Marinate the Chicken

    In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with the salt, black pepper, ginger, and mirin. Massage it
    well and let it sit for 20–30 minutes at room temperature.

  2. Prepare the Coating

    • In a separate bowl, mix the potato starch, flour, and baking soda.
    • Add the marinated chicken to the powder mixture. (If using an egg, beat the egg into the
      chicken before adding the powder).
    • Toss until every piece is thoroughly coated. The coating should feel dry and powdery on the
      outside; shake off any excess powder.

  3. The "Double-Fry" Technique

    Korean fried chicken is famous for being fried twice.

    • First Fry (Low Temp): Heat your oil to 320°F (160°C). Fry the chicken in batches for about
      5–7 minutes until lightly golden and cooked through. Remove and drain on a wire rack. Let
      the chicken rest for a few minutes to let the moisture steam off.
    • Second Fry (High Temp): Increase the oil heat to 375°F (190°C). Return the chicken to the
      oil and fry for another 2–3 minutes. The crust will turn dark golden brown and become
      extremely crunchy. Drain well.

  4. Make the Sauce

    • While the chicken is resting between fries, place a wok or large pan over medium-low heat.
    • Add a splash of oil and the minced garlic. Fry briefly until fragrant.
    • Add the Gochujang, ketchup, rice syrup, soy sauce, and sugar.
    • Stir constantly. Once the sauce starts bubbling and thickens slightly (about 3–4 minutes),
      remove it from the heat. Stir in the sesame oil.

  5. Toss and Glaze

    • Immediately toss the hot, double-fried chicken into the warm sauce.
    • Use a wooden spoon or spatula to mix gently but quickly until every piece is coated in the
      glossy red glaze.

  6. Serve

    • Transfer to a serving plate.
    • Sprinkle generously with toasted sesame seeds or crushed peanuts for texture.
    • Serve immediately with pickled radish cubes (Chicken-mu) to cut through the richness.
